Back then, the world I knew was within a small suburban area in Northeast Illinois. A trip to the city would take around thirty minutes, and to college about an hour. I never really felt just how short the distances are compared to how large my state is, or even how it compares to other states within the US as well as elsewhere in the world. Then everything changed when over the summer, I went with my family on a vacation to Poland. Residing in the city of Białystok, driving over to the countryside where many of my relatives lived took way more than an hour, and about the same time to drive to the nearby city of Augustów. My family and I also planned to visit Gdansk sometime in our vacation, but we didn't have the time to drive over there considering how far away it is. The area that we were exploring for two weeks was only a fraction of what Poland and its culture and history had to offer. Poland was clearly a very big country. “But how big?” I thought. Returning from vacation and with a month left of summer to spare, I began my work on a program to answer that very question. By the time I have finished coding it, my program ended up containing seven .csv files of all the worlds' nations plus all fifty US states, three classes for countries, regions, and the Geography Database, and my attempt at incorporating a graphical interface that responds to user input. This was an ambitious project that I worked on over the summer, and I still plan on adding more features to make it better.
